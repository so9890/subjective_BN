Under the joint policy, fossil research and non-energy research is higher. The reason is that the lower fossil tax allows for a higher fossil share in energy production so that fossil research is more profitable (the impact of he progressive income tax on fossil research is small but positive. Fossil research increases because the fossil labor good becomes relatively cheaper than the green one and energy producers increase their share of fossil goods. This makes fossil research more attractive. ) The reduction in green research is driven by the lower fossil tax (the progressive labor tax raises green research ). Over time, the difference to the policy regime without taul vanishes because the corrective taxes converge. The convergence becomes necessary as knowledge spillovers boost fossil production. 

Under the policy with progressive income tax scheme higher growth rates in all sectors are achieved. Especially growth in the fossil sector increases but also in the non-energy sector. 
Yet in the non-energy sector growth rate becomes smaller despite the lower fossil tax due to the progressive income tax which makes the energy good more expensive thereby directing research to the energy sector. 
Green growth deviates first negatively as less research is directed to the green sector when the lower fossil tax decreases the market size of green energy. However, \textbf{knowledge spillovers make the green sector profit from higher growth rates in the non-energy and fossil sector so that eventually the economy can profit from more green growth despite a lower fossil tax.} 

The green to fossil ratio decreases and the energy share rises under the joint policy. This could speak for a lower labor income tax to be optimal. 
Also consumption is lower.

\subsection{Equal capital shares}
When capital shares are equal, the necessary fossil tax reduces much more than with heterogeneous capital shares. The reason is that the supply side effect of a higher labor supply does not reduce green production costs. To meet the emission limit a more aggressive intervention is needed even though the progressive income tax lowers the level of economic activity. 

With equal capital shares non-energy growth and green growth is higher under the joint policy.
