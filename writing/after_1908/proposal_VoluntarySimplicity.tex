\section{Motivation}
Movements such as the Voluntary Simplicity Movement promotes a lifestyle of less consumption motivated by the need to reduce affluent consumption in developed countries to respect planetary boundaries.
HOW BIG, HOW IMPORTANT
VISIBLE IN eg DEMAND FOR DURABLES?
WHO ARE THESE PEOPLE? In terms of income and skills?

Such an intrinsic and voluntary reduction in consumption does not square with the monotonically increasing utility functions prominent in macroeconomic research. 
These models would predict that a negative shock to demand is, in the long-run, fully absorbed by a drop in prices and demand resurges to meet supply\footnote{\ Given an inelastic supply of labour. Under the assumption of elastic labour supply, labour supply reduces as demand becomes less important to the consumer.  }. 
It rather seems like, that there are costs associated with too high consumption levels. 
In light of voluntary simplicity the costs may be driven by environmental externalities or an moral obligation towards less developed countries. 

Macroeconomic research mainly focuses on supply-determined models to study environmental policies. This paper, in contrast, aims to lay special emphasis on the effects of demand.  \textit{It seems to suffice to allow for a labour supply choice on the household level. }
